About

Victoria is a thriving municipality in Laguna known for its duck-raising industry and rich local heritage.

With a growing population and a strong sense of community, Victoria is a place where progress meets tradition.

Brief History of Victoria, Laguna

Victoria was formerly a part of the town of Pila, Laguna, and became an independent municipality on November 15, 1949 through Executive Order No. 282 signed by then-President Elpidio Quirino. The town was named in honor of President Quirino’s daughter, Victoria Quirino, who was the First Lady at the time.

The municipality is famously known as the “Duck Raising Capital of the Philippines”, thanks to its thriving itik (native duck) industry. Over the years, Victoria has maintained its agricultural roots while steadily progressing toward modernization and improved public services.
