Ensuring Safety and Preparedness Through Emergency and Disaster Response in Victoria, Laguna

The Municipal Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (MDRRMO) of Victoria, Laguna is committed to ensuring the safety, preparedness, and resilience of the community during times of emergency and disaster. Whether facing natural calamities, accidents, or public health emergencies, the MDRRMO and its partners work around the clock to provide fast, efficient, and coordinated responses.

With a proactive approach to disaster risk reduction, the municipality conducts regular disaster preparedness drills, community education campaigns, and emergency response training. The goal is to empower residents with the knowledge and tools to stay safe and respond effectively during emergencies.

  • 24/7 Emergency Response Team through the MDRRMO hotline.
  • Rapid response to floods, typhoons, earthquakes, and fire incidents.
  • Well-equipped ambulances and trained first responders available anytime.
  • Ongoing community disaster preparedness education and drills.

In partnership with barangay officials, local health workers, and emergency units, the Victoria LGU aims to reduce the risks and impact of disasters. The MDRRMO also coordinates evacuation efforts and ensures the availability of relief goods and temporary shelters for affected families.

Victoria, Laguna continues to strengthen its disaster preparedness and response capabilities to safeguard the lives and welfare of its residents. With dedicated personnel, modern equipment, and a community-centered approach, the municipality is ready to face emergencies head-on and recover stronger.
