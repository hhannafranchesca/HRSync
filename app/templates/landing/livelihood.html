Livelihood in Victoria, Laguna

Victoria, Laguna is known as the "Duck Raising Capital of the Philippines" — a title that reflects its most iconic livelihood: itik (duck) farming. The town’s economy is deeply rooted in agriculture and animal husbandry, supporting many families through traditional and modern farming practices.

Beyond duck raising, residents also engage in rice farming, vegetable cultivation, and small-scale fishing. Cottage industries, such as weaving, native delicacies production, and handcrafted products, are also a vital part of Victoria’s thriving local economy.

  • Itik farming as a main source of income for many households.
  • Locally grown rice and vegetables supply nearby markets.
  • Handcrafted goods and local food products support small businesses.
  • Livelihood programs that empower women and youth through entrepreneurship.

The spirit of hard work and innovation keeps Victoria’s economy alive. With a strong sense of community and tradition, the people continue to evolve while holding on to the roots that define their way of life.
